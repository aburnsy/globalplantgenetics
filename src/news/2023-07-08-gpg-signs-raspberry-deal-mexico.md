Global Plant Genetics, a UK-based intellectual property firm, has extended its raspberry variety representation to Mexico and Guatemala. The expansion covers three varieties—Skye, Lewis, and Glen Mor—originally developed by James Hutton Ltd.

Jamie Petchell, head of raspberries at GPG, expressed enthusiasm about the development: "We have represented and developed these varieties in many other global territories for a number of years now. They provide a long season of supply of excellent quality fresh raspberries."

## Partnership Details

The company is collaborating with SynergiaBIO México, a nursery business operating modern cultivation facilities. Rolando Garcia, CEO of SynergiaBIO México, noted that the partnership aligns perfectly with their expansion efforts: "We are expanding our nursery facilities and in-vitro propagation in the country."

## About the Varieties

- **Skye:** A primocane type enabling double-cropping with high yields of firm, large berries suited for shipping
- **Lewis:** Known for sweet flavor and early primocane season productivity
- **Glen Mor:** A low-chilling floricane variety with natural Phytophthora root rot resistance

## Market Context

Mexico produces approximately 128,000 tonnes of raspberries annually, representing roughly 15% of global fresh raspberry exports. Production spans approximately 7,500 hectares, with 6,500 hectares utilizing plastic tunnel systems.
