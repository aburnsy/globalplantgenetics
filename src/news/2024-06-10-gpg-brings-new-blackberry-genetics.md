Global Plant Genetics (GPG) has finalized a licensing agreement with Niwa, a Polish Rubus breeding program located in Southern Poland, to represent two new primocane blackberry cultivars internationally: Maryna and Juhas. Both varieties feature self-fertility and thornless characteristics.

Bartek Danek, Niwa's Director, expressed enthusiasm about the partnership: "it has been important for our blackberry breeding efforts to develop primocane varieties...we look forward" to global expansion.

## Variety Characteristics

Agnieszka Orzel, the breeder, highlighted the cultivars' distinct strengths. She noted that "Maryna is better adapted to double cropping and that Juhas is more suitable for single primocane harvesting," emphasizing that self-fertility received careful attention during development.

## Market Potential

Jamie Petchell of GPG remarked on market potential, observing that "the global blackberry sector...has almost doubled in size over the last 20 years," positioning these new varieties to capitalize on growth opportunities through consistent, year-round quality supply.
