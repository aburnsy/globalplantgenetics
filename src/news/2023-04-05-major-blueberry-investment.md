Global Plant Genetics has acquired global licenses for three premium blueberry varieties from the University of Georgia: Sweet Duchess, Maverick, and Gladiator. These cultivars were developed by Dr. Scott NeSmith, whose breeding program is recognized for emphasizing yield, earliness, and flavor.

The deal finalized in early March, following years of international evaluation. The Maverick variety demonstrates exceptional performance in evergreen production systems, yielding approximately 4.78kg per plant in commercial settings just 16 months after planting. This productivity level has generated substantial interest among major blueberry producers in low-chill growing regions.

## Industry Impact

The company projects that Maverick will "be competing in the top 3 blueberry varieties in the world in the next 3-5 years." All three varieties showcase outstanding flavor, size, and yield characteristics.

## Availability

However, availability of plant material currently limits expansion. According to Rupert Hargreaves, "Forward planning for growers wanting access will be crucial" as international license holders work to increase production capacity.
