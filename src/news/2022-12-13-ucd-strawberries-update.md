Global Plant Genetics shared an encouraging update on UC Davis strawberry varieties, highlighting impressive performance metrics from their breeding program.

## Key Variety Performance

The UCD Royal Royce variety is achieving "4kgs per plant," demonstrating exceptional productivity. Two additional cultivars—UCD Valiant and UCD Moxie—are performing at notably high levels, positioning themselves as viable replacements for established commercial varieties Cabrillo and Monterrey.

Short-day varieties UCD Victor and UCD Warrior are generating marketplace enthusiasm. Looking ahead, the company plans to issue nursery licenses for day-neutral types UCD Finn and UCD Mojo, while simultaneously trialing five newly launched California varieties: UCD Surfline, UCD Monarch, UCD Golden Gate, UCD Eclipse, and UCD Keystone.

## Program Advances

The breeding initiative has made "massive leaps forward in disease resistance and flavour profiling." The organization expresses confidence that these genetics will "rapidly replace standard, older varieties."

## Industry Perspective

Jack Beattie from JCLM Farming, Australia, praised the varieties' performance: "UCD Moxie has impressed with early season yield and exceptional flavour" while "Royal Royce is marginally later but looking very strong into mid-season."
