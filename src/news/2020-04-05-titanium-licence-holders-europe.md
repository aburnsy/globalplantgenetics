Global Plant Genetics serves as the international Master Licence holder for Oregon Blueberry Farm and Nursery, representing two newly developed varieties. The Titanium blueberry represents a significant advancement in the Northern Highbush sector.

## Key Characteristics

The variety demands roughly 1000 chill hours annually and exhibits strong resilience in cold climates, having been developed in Oregon. Berries consistently measure 17-20mm with a light blue hue and distinctive bloom. Plants grow upright, making harvesting convenient.

Regarding timing, Titanium ripens "approximately 5-7 days later than Duke and slightly earlier than Draper." The berries demonstrate superior firmness compared to most competitors, delivering excellent flavor with a satisfying textural quality.

## European Licensees

The company has authorized several European nurseries to propagate Titanium, including Kusibab-Wyka, Planasa, Plants365, Schrijnwerkers, and Vivai Molari. Response has been positive, and increasing availability from licensed propagators is anticipated.

The organization notes that "Titanium certainly is a great breakthrough for those growers in the Fresh Market blueberry supply chain," encouraging early ordering for upcoming plantation seasons.
