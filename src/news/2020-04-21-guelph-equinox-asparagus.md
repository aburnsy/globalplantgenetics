Global Plant Genetics has introduced Guelph Equinox, an early-season asparagus hybrid that demonstrated exceptional performance during the 2020 harvest season. The variety combines early maturity, robust productivity, and superior spear quality, garnering attention from growers across Europe and beyond.

Caroline Hulland, an asparagus producer in Shropshire and chairman of Westgrowers, shared her experience: "The spears were extremely early to emerge, and they have a fantastic flavour. The variety is performing superbly under plastic."

## Breeding Background

The cultivar originated from breeding work by Dr. David Wolyn at the University of Guelph in Ontario, Canada. Following successful trials in North American and European locations, Fox Seeds Inc. secured exclusive seed production and distribution rights. Dr. Wolyn noted that "Equinox can be a productive cultivar in many areas" with demonstrated advantages over its predecessor, Guelph Millennium.

Ken Wall, chairman of Fox Seeds and Canada's largest asparagus producer, confirmed the variety's strong performance across North America and indicated plans for expanded planting as seed volumes increased.

## Market Availability

By spring 2020, Guelph Equinox seeds became widely available to European growers. Jamie Petchell, heading Global Plant Genetics' asparagus division, reported "excellent seed sales" and noted that "crowns for planting in spring 2021 are already in very short supply."
