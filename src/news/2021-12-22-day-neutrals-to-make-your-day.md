Global Plant Genetics announced three new day-neutral strawberry varieties developed by UC Davis that are entering commercial cultivation in 2022: UCD Royal Royce, UCD Moxie, and UCD Valiant.

## UCD Royal Royce

This variety demonstrates superior performance in California trials, characterized by "large, well flavoured, visually appealing fruit with a good sugar / acid balance and excellent shelf life." The berries are approximately 10% larger than San Andreas varieties. A significant advantage is minimal runner production in fruiting fields, substantially reducing labor costs for growers. The plants develop notably large crowns while maintaining productivity.

## UCD Moxie

This cultivar stands out for disease resistance, producing substantial strawberry volumes later in the season. Like Royal Royce, it generates few runners, offering economic benefits. The enhanced disease resistance addresses challenges in exhausted soils and areas with restricted sterilization options. Performance metrics show improvements in yields, fruit dimensions, and marketable percentages compared to control varieties.

## UCD Valiant

Distinguished by robust early-season output and sustained yield advantages throughout the growing cycle, this variety features "excellent early season production." Testing revealed fruit firmness 28% superior to Monterey—advantageous for export operations. The large crowns and disease resistance suggest potential success in organic cultivation.

## Distribution

Propagation licenses have been secured with premier nurseries across Latin America, Europe, Turkey, Africa, the Middle East, and Australia.
