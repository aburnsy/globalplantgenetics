Global Plant Genetics organized a South African grower expedition in mid-October to showcase new blueberry genetics. The organization collaborated with Top Fruit, their licensed partner for the University of Georgia blueberry program in South Africa.

The visiting group, representing seven nations, examined five trial locations across various South African growing regions. The experience generated enthusiasm for accelerating worldwide distribution of these emerging varieties.

## Featured Varieties

**Sweet Duchess** stood out for its exceptionally early maturation—approximately two weeks ahead of major competitors. The evergreen, low-chill selection demonstrated "large berries, exceptional flavour, firm, and high yield" characteristics.

**Maverick**, another evergreen variety, delivered remarkable productivity metrics. Plants achieved 500 grams seven months post-planting and reached approximately 2.5 kilograms after sixteen months—a noteworthy accomplishment within South Africa's cultivation framework. The fruit displays substantial size, firmness, and distinctive sweet flavor with balanced acidity.

**Gladiator** represents a newer development in the breeding pipeline, presenting very large fruits with "excellent bloom, incredible flavour huge yield profile."

Additional varieties under evaluation included Early Duchess, Blue Duchess, Ava, and Tropical Blue.
