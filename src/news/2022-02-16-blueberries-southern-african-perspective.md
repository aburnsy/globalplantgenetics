Top Fruit, a South African varietal development company, has partnered with Global Plant Genetics since 2017 to commercialize blueberry varieties from the University of Georgia across Southern Africa. Pieter Zietsman, Berry Portfolio Manager at Top Fruit, recently discussed the company's progress and insights about the breeding program.

## Quality and Regional Adaptation

Top Fruit prioritizes firm berries capable of withstanding long-distance shipping. Zietsman emphasized that the ideal characteristics include "a crunchy pop sensation" rather than fibrous texture. The company targets fruit sizes between 14-25mm, which satisfy most market preferences. Importantly, Zietsman noted that "we do not select varieties for machine-harvesting" but instead prefer open, spreading bush structures suited to manual harvesting operations.

Southern Africa's diverse climate zones—from the Mediterranean Western Cape to subtropical northern regions—present adaptation challenges. Top Fruit has identified low-to-very-low chill varieties for warmer areas and evergreen types for no-chill regions like Zambia and Kenya.

## Top Performing Varieties

Zietsman identified three standout performers for global potential: Sweet Duchess, Maverick, and Gladiator. Sweet Duchess produces early fruit with excellent quality, achieving approximately "20 tons per hectare within three years." Maverick demonstrates vigorous growth with natural feathering and flowers early, while Gladiator offers dwarfing characteristics with abundant flower buds and excellent fruit quality.

The pipeline continues to advance, promising additional commercial opportunities ahead.
