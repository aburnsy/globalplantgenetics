As the early growing season winds down across the Northern Hemisphere, Global Plant Genetics reflects on the strong performance of its low-chill blueberry portfolio in 2025. The company reports visiting major production regions worldwide and witnessing impressive results across its varietal range.

## Variety Performance

**Tropical Blue** leads the early-season offerings, demonstrating "an unrivalled combination of early production and excellent yields of high-quality berries." Spanish growers are employing specialized pruning techniques to concentrate ripening, allowing harvests during peak market pricing while reducing labor costs.

**Sweet Duchess** maintains its reputation for premium quality, offering "very large, firm and sweet berries" that appeal to top-tier retailers. Its arrival just after Tropical Blue fills a critical supply window with distinctive fruit characteristics.

**Gladiator** provides flexibility for harvest timing through strategic pruning management. The variety delivers consistent large fruits with superior flavor and sugar-acid balance, earning recognition as a grower and retailer favorite across the supply chain.

**Maverick** continues setting yield records, now demonstrating performance in Asian and North African markets. The variety achieves "over 4kgs / plant one year after planting," with mature plantations reaching 6+ kilograms annually while maintaining 18-20mm berry size with excellent flavor profiles.

The company signals forthcoming innovations, noting an "exciting pipeline" of next-generation low-chill varieties under development.
