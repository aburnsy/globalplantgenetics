Global Plant Genetics (GPG) has reached a significant milestone, marking a decade of achievements in plant breeding and intellectual property. The company was established by Jamie Petchell and Rupert Hargreaves, combining over fifty years of horticultural knowledge between them.

The founding vision centered on becoming the world's leading IP company in its product range, providing superior genetics and expertise spanning from cultivation to consumer markets. The organization has developed a specialist international team covering plant breeding, IP protection, licensing, technical support, and market development.

## Global Success

GPG's success stems from maintaining close relationships with clients and breeders across multiple continents—from European fields to Asian production hubs. This global presence has enabled the company to develop a diverse crop portfolio reflecting current grower and market demands. Products are recognized for yield performance, climate resilience, flavor profiles, and innovation.

## Queen's Award Recognition

A significant achievement came in 2022 when GPG received the Queen's Award for Enterprise: International Trade, recognizing the company's excellence in international commerce.

## Looking Forward

Looking forward, GPG intends to expand its variety portfolio while addressing global demand for fresh, nutritious produce. Berry cultivation remains central to the company's mission, with ongoing investment in breeding programs and grower assistance.

Petchell reflected: "We wouldn't be where we are today without the dedication and support" of the global network. Hargreaves added that "the past ten years have been an incredible journey."
