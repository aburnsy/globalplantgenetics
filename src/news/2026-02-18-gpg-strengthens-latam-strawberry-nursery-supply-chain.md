Global Plant Genetics (GPG) has licensed two Argentine nurseries—Kleppe S.A. and Vivero Altus—to address growing demand for premium strawberry plants across Latin America.

Both operations will cultivate established varieties including San Andreas and Fronteras, while introducing advanced replacements. Three day-neutral varieties receive particular focus: UCD Royal Royce, UC Eclipse, and UC Golden Gate, which promise "critical improvements over current market leaders."

## The Nurseries

Vivero Altus, based in El Maitén, Patagonia, is led by Laureano Delarmelina and Juan Vilar, who bring extensive strawberry production expertise. Kleppe S.A., Argentina's second-largest fruit exporter, operates from Neuquén with 3,700 employees across 9,000 hectares.

Delarmelina emphasized that "the new replacement varieties offer significant improvements in areas such as yield, disease resistance, and shelf life." Kleppe owner Pablo Kleppe highlighted his firm's readiness, stating the company possesses "high-quality sandy land in the right location, multiple warehouses, and extensive cold storage facilities."

## Market Impact

UC-developed varieties currently dominate over 90% of Latin America's strawberry market, positioning these nurseries to capture substantial growth opportunities.
