The 2024 Northern European asparagus season has experienced significant volatility driven primarily by weather patterns. Heavy rainfall during autumn and winter months elevated disease risks in both new and established plantings. Late March brought warmer conditions and strong initial harvests, but cool easterly winds in early April suppressed yields and supply volumes.

Production began recovering toward month-end, with current output exceeding 2023 levels during the same period. Weather forecasts suggest stable conditions ahead, which should enable consistent supply through peak season.

## Key Developments

The global asparagus industry has witnessed reduced production acreage over five years, yet this contraction has paradoxically strengthened fresh product sales. Consequently, grower confidence in new plantings has rebounded over the past year.

As a long-term perennial crop, asparagus requires strategic planning around production and marketing decisions. Global Plant Genetics supports growers by identifying varieties that optimize profitability through superior yields, spear quality, and favorable harvest timing.

The organization anticipates strengthening market conditions throughout the second half of the season for European asparagus producers and marketers.
