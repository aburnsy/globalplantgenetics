The blueberry variety Tropical Blue is exhibiting notably advanced phenology this season in both Spain and Morocco, positioning it as one of the earliest commercially productive cultivars currently being grown in these regions. Observations from multiple commercial sites indicate that Tropical Blue is entering harvest significantly ahead of standard early-season varieties, with growers reporting one of the earliest fruiting windows recorded to date for Mediterranean environments.

This accelerated ripening provides clear strategic benefits within early-market supply programmes. Early-season availability is particularly valuable given the limited global supply during the Northern Hemisphere winter, enabling producers to access favourable pricing while providing retailers with consistent volumes of high-grade fruit during a critical supply gap.

In addition to its phenological advantage, Tropical Blue continues to deliver the fruit-quality attributes documented in earlier trials. Berries demonstrate high uniformity, large average fruit size, elevated firmness, and stable flavour characteristics, supported by strong postharvest performance. These traits contribute to reduced sorting losses and improved packout percentages, making the variety suitable for both domestic distribution and long-distance export markets.

Yield potential is emerging as another key differentiator. Commercial blocks at 2.5 years of age are currently forecast to produce approximately 6 kg of Class 1 fruit per plant this season. This performance level is considered significant for a young planting and suggests strong productive capacity as the cultivar matures. Preliminary projections indicate that yields are likely to continue increasing through years 3–5 as canopy structure and root systems fully develop.

With its combination of ultra-early fruiting, consistent high fruit quality, and promising yield metrics, Tropical Blue is establishing itself as a technically robust option for growers aiming to strengthen early-season supply programmes in Mediterranean production zones.
