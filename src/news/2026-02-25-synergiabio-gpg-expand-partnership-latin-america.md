Two companies—SynergiaBIO (based in Chile) and Global Plant Genetics (UK)—have joined forces to advance raspberry and blackberry development throughout South and Central America. The collaboration expands on their earlier work distributing varieties from The James Hutton Institute, a premier breeding center in the UK.

Under this expanded arrangement, SynergiaBIO takes on a more active development role. The organization will contribute expertise in variety testing, regional trials, and production scaling, while simultaneously helping shape breeding objectives from the outset. The goal centers on identifying genetics tailored to diverse climatic zones in the region, emphasizing traits like productivity, fruit dimensions, durability, and taste alongside disease resistance needed for modern export operations.

## Partnership Vision

Rolando Garcia, SynergiaBIO's owner, stated that "structured development and strong technical support are for growers," emphasizing how this phase enables longer-term genetic initiatives benefiting regional producers.

Jamie Petchell from GPG commented that "new raspberry and blackberry cultivars are carefully selected, locally adapted" for lasting commercial viability in Latin America's expanding berry sector.

Trial initiatives are anticipated to launch imminently, demonstrating both organizations' dedication to responsible expansion and intellectual property safeguards throughout the region's berry industry.
