Global Plant Genetics announced the market arrival of Titanium® Blueberry, a promising new cultivar developed by Oregon Blueberry Farms and Nursery. The variety represents a significant advancement in blueberry breeding, combining multiple desirable characteristics for commercial growers.

## Key Characteristics

The Titanium variety demonstrates impressive horticultural traits. According to the announcement, it is "a new high chill, early, large fruited, sweet flavoured, firm and crisp blueberry variety." Additional strengths include uniform ripening, exceptional storage capacity, upright growth habit, and suitability for mechanical harvesting.

## Performance Metrics

The berries measure 17-20 mm in diameter and ripen approximately one week after Duke varieties. The cultivar requires over 1000 chill hours and has shown strong winter hardiness in the Pacific Northwest. Most impressively, yields reportedly exceed 20 tonnes per hectare.

## Market Outlook

Global Plant Genetics expressed confidence in the variety's potential, stating they anticipated it would become "a major international blueberry variety in the near future." The company is actively seeking licensing agreements across multiple territories, reflecting strong interest from international contacts.
