Global Plant Genetics reports progress on new blackberry cultivars following a representation agreement with Polish partner Niwa. The Juhas and Maryna primocane blackberry varieties are undergoing field trials across multiple regions, with initial harvests expected later this year.

Results from Polish growing operations show impressive fruit characteristics, with Maryna performing well as a dual-cropping option and Juhas excelling in single-season primocane systems.

## Breeding Focus

Agnieszka Orzel, breeder at Niwa, emphasized the importance of self-fertility in variety development: "Self-fertility is oft forgotten in Rubus breeding but it can have a huge impact" on yields in warmer climates. She highlighted that Maryna suits double-cropping operations while Juhas adapts better to single primocane harvesting systems.

Both varieties feature thornless canes and high self-fertility, designed to perform well across major global blackberry growing regions.
